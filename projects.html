Project 01

Climate Change Mitigation & Environmental Conservation Initiative

Emmanuel Humanitarian Foundation (EHF) distributed 100 olive seedlings to households and community stakeholders in Mijilu Community, Mubi North Local Government Area of Adamawa State. The initiative promoted tree planting, environmental conservation, and community awareness on climate change adaptation.

Community members, youth groups, women groups, and local leaders participated in the exercise and received guidance on proper planting and maintenance of the seedlings. The activity strengthened local participation in environmental protection and encouraged shared responsibility for preserving natural resources.

Project 02

Menstrual Hygiene Awareness & Sanitary Pad Distribution

On 6 December 2025, Emmanuel Humanitarian Foundation (EHF) conducted a menstrual hygiene awareness campaign and distributed 50 sanitary pads to adolescent girls and young women in Pariye Community, Maiha Local Government Area of Adamawa State.

The intervention improved menstrual health knowledge, promoted proper personal hygiene practices, and encouraged open discussion around menstruation. Participants received guidance on menstrual health management, proper use and disposal of sanitary pads, hand hygiene, and breaking stigma and misconceptions associated with menstruation.

Project 03

Humanitarian & Peacebuilding Visit to Anguwan Rukuba

On 20 April 2026, Emmanuel Humanitarian Foundation (EHF) conducted a humanitarian and peacebuilding visit to Anguwan Rukuba in Jos North Local Government Area, Plateau State, following a recent attack that affected the community.

The visit engaged women and young girls in discussions on peacebuilding, conflict prevention, social cohesion, and community resilience. Participants received education on peaceful coexistence, dialogue, non-violent conflict resolution, and the critical role of women and girls in promoting peace and stability within families and communities.
